Chapter 1: Light – Reflection and Refraction

Concept Notes

• Light: Electromagnetic energy that enables vision. Travels in straight lines in a homogeneous medium :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}.

• Laws of Reflection: (1) Angle of incidence = angle of reflection; (2) Incident ray, normal, and reflected ray lie in the same plane :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}.

• Refraction and Refractive Index: Bending of light at boundary between media; refractive index = speed of light in air / speed in medium :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}.

• Mirrors and Lenses: Spherical mirrors (concave and convex), lenses; focal length f = R/2 ; power = 1/f (in meters) :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}.

Exercise 10.1 (NCERT In-text Questions)

Q1. Define the principal focus of a concave mirror.
Light rays parallel to the principal axis converge at a point on the axis after reflection – the principal focus :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}.
Q2. The radius of curvature of a spherical mirror is 20 cm. What is its focal length?
f = R/2 = 20 cm / 2 = 10 cm :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}.
Q3. Name a mirror which gives an erect and enlarged image of an object.
A concave mirror (when object is between pole and focus) gives a virtual, erect, enlarged image :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}.
Q4. Why is a convex mirror preferred as a rear‑view mirror?
It gives an erect, diminished, virtual image with a wide field of view :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}.

Exercise 10.2 (NCERT End-of-Chapter)

Q1. Focal length of a convex mirror whose radius is 32 cm?
f = R/2 = 32 cm / 2 = 16 cm :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}.
Q2. A concave mirror produces 3× magnified real image of an object placed at 10 cm in front. Where is the image?
Magnification = v/u; here v = 3 × (−10 cm) = −30 cm; image is real and 30 cm in front of mirror :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}.
